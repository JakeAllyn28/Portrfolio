# AR Scanning & Interactive Experiences

**Context:** Personal Projects  
**Stack:** Unity, C#, ARCore / Vuforia  
**Focus:** Augmented Reality, Interaction Design  

---

## Overview

Developed multiple AR prototypes exploring interactive experiences, spatial content placement, and educational applications using image tracking and ground-plane detection.

Projects included AR sports simulations, home visualization tools, and an educational “holographic textbook” concept.

---

## AR Sports & AR Home Prototypes

### Concept

Explored AR-based interaction systems for:

- Sports simulations (pool, ping pong)  
- Home visualization (placing furniture and interactive objects)  

Used ground-plane detection to place objects realistically within the environment.

---

## Problem

- Mobile AR interaction is constrained by single-handed input  
- Precise control in 3D space is difficult without additional input devices  
- Fast-paced interactions (e.g., ping pong) felt clunky and unresponsive  

---

## Results

- Pool-style interactions were viable and felt reasonably natural  
- Fast-reaction gameplay (ping pong) was not practical with current input constraints  
- Identified core limitations of mobile AR interaction design  

---

## Pocket Realm (WIP)

### Concept

An educational AR platform where physical objects act as interactive “textbooks.”

- Trading cards function as entry points to content  
- Scanning a card instantiates an interactive 3D scene  
- Designed for educational environments (e.g., animals, environments, systems)  

---

## Key Features

- Image tracking for content activation  
- Interactive 3D scenes with contextual information  
- Expandable system for multiple “card-based” experiences  

---

## Technical Details

- Hybrid use of image tracking and ground-plane detection  
- Modular scene instantiation pipeline  
- Designed for scalable content expansion  

---

## Results / Impact

- Explored limitations of AR interaction design  
- Identified viable use cases (education, slower-paced interactions)  
- Developed foundation for ongoing “Pocket Realms” project
