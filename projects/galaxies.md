# Galactic Interaction Simulation

**Context:** UC Irvine (Undergraduate Research)  
**Stack:** Python, C++, N-body simulation tools (Rebound, GADGET-2, AMUSE)  
**Focus:** Astrophysics Simulation, Orbital Dynamics  

---

## Overview

Studied large-scale gravitational interactions between the Milky Way, the Andromeda Galaxy, and their satellite galaxies.

Focused on explaining observed orbital patterns of Milky Way satellite galaxies using multi-body simulation and theoretical models.

---

## Problem

- Observed satellite galaxy orbits exhibited high eccentricity and unusual distributions  
- Standard gravitational models did not fully explain these behaviors  
- Needed to evaluate long-term orbital evolution under multi-body interactions  

---

## Hypothesis

Explored the hypothesis that the Kozai–Lidov (KL) mechanism influenced satellite galaxy orbits.

If the Milky Way and Andromeda are treated as a coupled system, their gravitational interaction could induce KL oscillations in satellite galaxies, leading to highly eccentric orbits.

---

## Approach

- Modeled Milky Way, Andromeda, and satellite galaxies as interacting gravitational bodies  
- Used N-body simulation tools to evaluate orbital evolution over time  
- Compared point-mass approximations and more detailed models  
- Analyzed orbital parameters (eccentricity, inclination, stability)  

---

## Key Features

- Multi-body gravitational simulation  
- Long-timescale orbital evolution analysis  
- Comparison of theoretical models vs simulated behavior  
- Exploration of Kozai–Lidov effects in galactic systems  

---

## Results / Insights

- Demonstrated that multi-body interactions could produce highly eccentric satellite orbits  
- Provided evidence supporting KL-like mechanisms in galactic-scale systems  
- Improved understanding of satellite galaxy distribution patterns  

---

## Technical Contributions

- Built and ran simulation workflows in Python and C++  
- Configured and extended existing astrophysics simulation tools  
- Performed data analysis on orbital outputs
